Movies are rated by various organizations, such as the MPAA (Motion Picture Association of America) in the U.S. and the BBFC (British Board of Film Classification) in the U.K. Ratings are determined based on factors like language, violence, and sexual content. Common ratings include G (General), PG (Parental Guidance), PG-13 (Parents Strongly Cautioned), R (Restricted), and NC-17 (Adults Only).

Major film awards include the Academy Awards (Oscars), Golden Globe Awards, BAFTA Awards, and Cannes Film Festival Awards. These awards recognize excellence in various categories such as Best Picture, Best Director, and Best Actor/Actress. Winning or being nominated for these awards can significantly impact a film's success and the careers of those involved.

Influential directors include Steven Spielberg, Alfred Hitchcock, Martin Scorsese, and Quentin Tarantino. Notable producers include George Lucas and Harvey Weinstein. These individuals have shaped the film industry with their groundbreaking work and have had a lasting impact on cinema.

Common terms include: Blockbuster (a highly successful and popular film), Pre-production (the phase before filming begins, including planning and casting), Post-production (the phase after filming, including editing and sound design), Script (the written text of a film, including dialogue and stage directions).

Movies often reflect societal values, issues, and trends, such as fashion, politics, and social norms. For example, films like "The Matrix" reflect technological anxieties, while movies like "The Breakfast Club" address teenage angst and social issues.

Notable plot twists include the reveal of the killer in "The Sixth Sense," the twist ending of "Fight Club," and the true identity of Keyser Söze in "The Usual Suspects." These twists are memorable for their impact on the storyline and audience perception.

Technology has revolutionized film-making with advancements such as CGI (Computer-Generated Imagery), digital cameras, and sophisticated editing software. These innovations have enabled more complex visual effects, improved film quality, and streamlined production processes.

Iconic filming locations include the Shire in "The Lord of the Rings," filmed in New Zealand, and the "Star Wars" deserts of Tunisia. These locations often become tourist attractions and are known for their distinctive appearances in the films.

Movies can evoke a wide range of emotions, such as joy, sadness, fear, or excitement. They can also influence psychological responses, like empathy or stress. Filmmakers use various techniques, such as music and cinematography, to enhance emotional impact.

Some of the most expensive films include "Avengers: Endgame," "Pirates of the Caribbean: On Stranger Tides," and "Avatar." High costs often result from extensive special effects, star-studded casts, and elaborate sets. These films require significant budgets to achieve their ambitious visions.
